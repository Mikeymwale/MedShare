Zam-MedShare prevents medicine waste and redistributes it to patients who need it most.

The Crisis We're Solving

1 in 4 Zambians struggles to access essential medicine, while millions of dollars' worth of medicine goes to waste each year. No one should have to choose between food, rent, and their health.

"Every expired medicine in a pharmacy could have saved a life in our communities."

How We Save Lives

Our simple three-step process transforms medicine waste into life-saving care

1. Collect

Pharmacies, hospitals, and individuals donate surplus medicine that would otherwise expire unused.

2. Verify

Zam-MedShare carefully verifies, sorts, and matches medicine to ensure safety and effectiveness.

3. Deliver

Community partners deliver medicine directly to patients who need it most in underserved areas.

About Zam-MedShare

We believe that access to medicine is a fundamental human right, not a privilege.

Our Mission

To eliminate medicine waste in Zambia while ensuring that every person has access to the life-saving medications they need, regardless of their economic circumstances.

Our Vision

"A Zambia where no medicine goes to waste, and no patient goes without care."

We envision a future where the gap between medicine surplus and medicine need is completely eliminated through innovative redistribution systems and community partnerships.

Our Values

Compassion for every patient
Transparency in all operations
Innovation in healthcare access
Sustainability for the future

Our Story

Zam-MedShare was born from a simple yet powerful observation: while families in rural Zambia were struggling to afford basic medications, pharmacies and hospitals in urban areas were disposing of thousands of dollars worth of unexpired medicine.

Our founders witnessed firsthand how this disconnect was creating unnecessary suffering. Children were going without antibiotics, diabetics were rationing insulin, and elderly patients were choosing between food and their heart medication.

"We realized that the solution wasn't just about charity – it was about creating a sustainable system that benefits everyone."

Today, Zam-MedShare is transforming how Zambia thinks about medicine distribution, creating a model that other countries are beginning to adopt.
